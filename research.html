Who Should Get Money? Estimating Welfare Weights in the U.S. (with Francesco Capozza )

We identify the welfare weights assigned by the general population of the U.S. to individuals in society using a large-scale real-stakes experiment. Welfare weights measure the value of providing an additional dollar of consumption to individuals in society. These weights are sufficient statistics that can incorporate various ideals and can be directly used to evaluate reforms. We find that the welfare weights of the general population of the U.S. (i) exhibit, on average, a low to moderate degree of progressivity, (ii) predict their policy preferences, (iii) are slightly more progressive than the welfare weights implied by the existing policies in the U.S., and (iv) are much less progressive than the welfare weights commonly used in the literature.
